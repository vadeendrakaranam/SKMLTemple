Temple History

Sri Kanaka Mahalakshmi Ammavaru Temple, located in the historic Burujupeta area of Visakhapatnam, Andhra Pradesh, is one of the most ancient and revered temples in the region. Based on local traditions and historical evidence, the temple is believed to date back to the 11th century. According to legend, during an excavation in the area centuries ago, a sacred idol of the goddess emerged mysteriously from the earth. The local people recognized it as a swayambhu (self-manifested) form of Goddess Kanaka Mahalakshmi. From that moment on, the deity has been worshipped as the Grama Devata (presiding deity) of Visakhapatnam. It is also believed that the temple received patronage during the rule of the great Vijayanagara Empire, reflecting its historical and cultural importance. Devotees have long believed that the goddess safeguards the city and bestows blessings of peace, prosperity, and protection upon her people.

A unique feature of this temple is that the idol of the goddess is installed in the open, without a traditional sanctum or roof, allowing devotees direct and uninterrupted darshan at all times. This symbolizes the goddess's ever-accessible and compassionate nature. The temple witnesses heavy footfall, especially on Fridays, and even more so during the auspicious month of Margasira (November‚ÄìDecember), which is considered sacred to the goddess. Thousands of devotees from across Andhra Pradesh and nearby states participate in the Margasira Lakshmi Vratams, seeking her divine blessings for wealth, happiness, and well-being. It is widely believed that prayers offered with devotion at this temple help fulfill wishes and bring prosperity into one‚Äôs life. Over the centuries, Sri Kanaka Mahalakshmi Ammavaru has become more than a spiritual symbol ‚Äî she is regarded as the guardian deity and cultural pride of Visakhapatnam.
